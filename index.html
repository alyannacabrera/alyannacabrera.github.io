The Research Problem

While standard Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) excels at simple fact retrieval, it struggles with complex institutional processes that require multi-source synthesis. Traditional RAG relies on isolated text chunks; our research proposes a Knowledge Graph (KG) approach to capture interconnected procedural nodes and edges.

The Focus Mismatch

Most KG research targets static, encyclopedic facts. We defined a new model specifically for dynamic process data (steps, prerequisites, references).

The Creation Bottleneck

Building KGs usually requires manual expert intervention. We developed an automated pipeline to transform raw documents into structured graph data.

Figure 1: End-to-End Pipeline Automated Extraction, Multi-Turn Validation, and Scope-Based Retrieval.

Technical Implementation

We engineered a two-stage framework: Automatic KG Creation and Scope-Based Question Answering.

01. KG Construction

Utilized Gemini 2.0 Flash with multi-turn validation prompting. The system reviews its own JSON output for completeness, correcting logic errors (e.g., incorrect step sequences) before insertion into Neo4j via Cypher queries.

02. Scope-Based QA

Implemented a traversal strategy where the LLM selects "Scopes"pre-defined Cypher strategiesto explore neighboring nodes, ensuring the context includes related prerequisites and contact info.

Figure 2: Neo4j Process Subgraph Visualization.
Figure 3: Multi-turn Validation Logic for Accuracy.

Evaluation & Results

Tested against a custom gold-standard dataset of 1,467 nodes and 90 complex Q&A pairs derived from real university institutional data.

93.14% Node Generation Accuracy
93.52% QA System F1-Score
4.35 / 5.0 User Satisfaction (BUS-15)

Key Insights

  • High Recall (94.86%): The graph-based retrieval successfully captured nearly all relevant facts compared to traditional RAG.
  • Validation Impact: Multi-turn self-correction by the LLM significantly reduced logic errors in process ordering.
  • UX Strengths: Students highlighted the system's superior ability to provide explicit citations and contact resources.
